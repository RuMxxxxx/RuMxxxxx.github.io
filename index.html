Golf Venues

Guangzhou Fengshen Golf Club – Dye Course

  • 18-HOLE PRIVATE MOUNTAIN COURSE
  • PETE DYE FAMILY DESIGN
  • HAWAII PLATINUM GRASS GREENS
  • 8500 SQ.M LUXURY CLUBHOUSE

Course Overview

The Dye Course is an ingeniously crafted and delightfully challenging mountain course, designed by the renowned Pete Dye family of the United States, often hailed as the "uncanny master" of golf course design. The lead architect, Cynthia Dye, has deeply captured the essence of the Dye design style. Opened in 2009, the course features 18 holes, a par of 72, a total length of 6,680 yards, a course rating of 72.2, and a slope rating of 138.As a private course for Fengshen members, the designer fully considered the harmonious coexistence of the undulating natural mountain scenery and the fairways during the design and construction process, embodying a sense of softness and elegance.

Design Philosophy

The course carries forward the Dye family's signature style of "determining the fairway by the green," infusing each hole with feminine softness and the playful spirit of the game. The bunkers are exquisitely crafted, like mischievous little sprites that conceal hidden dangers, ready to tease golfers at any moment.The greens are planted with Hawaii Platinum Grass, and the fairways feature Hawaii Grass, ensuring exceptional quality.While the overall distance is not extremely long, the fairways are narrow, requiring golfers to carefully strategize their shots, making it an ideal course for beginner to intermediate players seeking improvement.

Clubhouse & Facilities

The club features an 8,500-square-meter New Asian-style golf clubhouse designed by the world-renowned KTGY firm. The saddle-shaped roof, which slopes down from front to back, creates a striking visual impact, while the extensive use of glass curtain walls allows ample natural light in all functional areas, reflecting a strong commitment to low-carbon environmental awareness. The lobby lounge, restaurant, locker area, and banquet hall all offer 270-degree views of the course.Additionally, the club boasts a nearly 2,000-square-meter putting green, a chipping practice area featuring various slopes and bunker types, and the Fengshen Golf Academy, equipped with world-leading technological training facilities.

Guangzhou Nansha Golf Club – Lake Course

  • LAKESIDE ECOLOGICAL COURSE
  • 9-HOLE LIGHTED NIGHT GOLF
  • SEASIDE LOCATION IN NANSHA
  • LUXURY VILLA ACCOMMODATION

Course Overview

Guangzhou Nansha Golf Club is nestled within the ecological green landscape of Nansha District, Guangzhou. It was master-planned under the visionary leadership of patriotic entrepreneur Sir Henry Fok, embodying the ambition to create a 21st-century seaside new town.The club was designed by John Jacobs and officially opened in 1997.The Lake Course (also known as Luwan Course) is located in the valley flatlands, with fairways extending westward along the hillside. It stretches 6,626 yards and has a par of 72.Thanks to the distinctive terrain, the fairways feature undulating contours and abundant variety, offering rich and enjoyable play.

Design Philosophy

The course is built along the mountains, offering an excellent natural environment. The back 18 holes (Huangshanlu Course) are located to the north of the valley, generally situated at higher elevations.The course follows the route of a high-end resort and private club — bunkers are not overly deep, and the difficulty is not as demanding as tournament-style courses. However, because the fairways stretch toward the mountains and are mostly uneven, the course inherently presents a certain level of challenge, which is where its appeal lies.Notably, the par-4 11th hole features a fairway built on an artificial island, offering opportunities to land in the water whether teeing off or approaching the green — a truly treacherous challenge.

Clubhouse & Facilities

Club facilities include both water and land practice ranges, an indoor tennis hall, a hotel, villa guest rooms, and a 7,000-square-meter clubhouse building. The clubhouse is conveniently located near the 9th and 18th greens of both courses. Interior facilities include Chinese and Western restaurants, an open-air tea pavilion, steam saunas, hot and cold massage rooms, a fitness center, as well as a multi-function banquet hall and private VIP rooms.The course also features a 9-hole lighted night course that encircles the mountaintop, composed of different greens and fairways from the back 18 holes, offering a truly distinctive experience.

Shenzhen Noble Merchant Golf Club

  • DOWNTOWN SHENZHEN LOCATION
  • 18-HOLE PROFESSIONAL NIGHT COURSE
  • CASTLE-STYLE CLUBHOUSE
  • STATE BANQUET CUISINE SERVICE

Course Overview

Shenzhen Noble Merchant Golf Club is a standard 18-hole lighted golf course, conveniently located in the heart of Shenzhen along Shennan Avenue. Opened in 1996, the course covers approximately 500,000 square meters with gently rolling fairways and pleasant surroundings. Total length is 5,968 yards, par 72, designed by Australian architect Les Watts. For busy golfers, the club's greatest value lies in its night-optimized design - a comprehensive lighting system covers every fairway and green, providing ample brightness without casting distracting shadows during your swing.

Night Golf Highlights

The night course at Noble Merchant is not merely “adding lights” – the night experience was considered from the very beginning of the design. The lighting system is carefully arranged to be even and gentle, allowing you to clearly see fairway undulations, green contours, and the edges of water hazards. The back nine holes are especially impressive at night – the long, wide fairways take on a dramatic appearance under the lights, complemented by well designed greens and bunkers, allowing both professional and amateur golfers to appreciate the unique charm of night golf. The 7th hole is a classic on the night course: a 132 yard par 3 with the green completely surrounded by water and bunkers front and back. The lights reflect off the water, creating a striking visual effect and offering a severe test of distance and accuracy. After finishing 18 holes, you can dine or relax in the castle style clubhouse without feeling rushed.

Clubhouse & Facilities

The castle style clubhouse, designed by Britain‘s PMJM, looks especially elegant under the night lights. Facilities include deluxe suites, Chinese and Western restaurants, a bar, a fitness center, and a golf shop. The driving range also offers night service with professional instructors. A lighted standard tennis court is also available. Notably, the Noble Merchant State Banquet Hall specializes in state banquet cuisine, perfect for entertaining or rewarding yourself after a round. In short, this club is designed for “a relaxed round after work.”
